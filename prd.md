# Product Requirements Document: Museum Artwork Explorer

## 1. Introduction & Vision

This document outlines the product requirements for the "Museum Artwork Explorer," a website designed to help users discover and explore museum artworks, exhibitions, and galleries.

The vision is to provide a simple, intuitive, and map-centric platform for tourists to easily find art they are interested in, identify the museums that house it, and discover other nearby cultural attractions.

## 2. Target Audience

*   **Primary Audience:** Tourists
*   **Key Characteristics:**
    *   Often use mobile devices while on the go, making a mobile-first, responsive design essential.
    *   May have a casual interest in art rather than deep domain knowledge.
    *   Value location-based discovery to find attractions near them.
    *   Need quick access to practical information.

## 3. Goals

*   **User Goal:** To quickly search for specific artworks, see where they are located, and discover interesting museums and exhibitions.
*   **Business Goal:** To build an engaging and user-friendly platform that encourages art discovery and drives foot traffic to museums.

## 4. Features & Requirements

### P0 (Must-Have)

*   **Artwork Search:** The homepage will feature a prominent search bar allowing users to search for artworks by title. Search results will clearly display the artwork and link to the museum where it is located.
*   **Interactive Map View:** An interactive map will display the locations of all museums. Clicking on a museum's pin will show an information card with its name and city, with a link to the full museum page.
*   **Museum Pages:** Each museum will have a dedicated page showing its name, founding year, and lists of its current exhibitions and permanent collection.
*   **Exhibition Pages:** Each exhibition will have a dedicated page showing its name, duration (start/end dates), and a gallery of all artworks included in it.
*   **Artwork Pages:** Each artwork will have a page displaying its full details (title, artist, style, year created) and its precise location within a museum (e.g., Room, Floor).
*   **Responsive Design:** The website will be fully optimized for a seamless experience on all devices, including desktops, tablets, and smartphones.

### P1 (Should-Have)

*   **Artist Pages:** Dedicated pages for each artist, listing their biographical information and a gallery of their artworks.
*   **Advanced Search & Filtering:** Enhance the search functionality to allow users to filter results by artist, art style, or city.
*   **Geolocation:** The map will have an option to automatically center on the user's current location to simplify discovering nearby museums.

### P2 (Could-Have)

*   **Curator Pages:** Pages with information about museum curators and the exhibitions they have organized.
*   **User Favorites:** A feature allowing registered users to save their favorite artworks, artists, or museums for future reference.

## 5. Out of Scope

*   Integration with ticketing or booking systems.
*   Social media sharing features.
*   User-generated content such as reviews, ratings, or comments.
